# Pacific Vector: The Intelligence Workshop Concept

> This describes how we build, in service of the vision in `BUSINESS_CONCEPT.md`. For current build status and how this maps to phases, see `ROADMAP.md`.

Pacific Vector should not try to define its final commercial product too early. At this stage, the right goal is to build the underlying intelligence machinery: a flexible system that can produce increasingly sophisticated outputs over time.

The daily newsletter is the first product, but it is also the testing ground. If built properly, every daily brief strengthens the system behind it.

## The Core Machinery

1. **Source ingestion** — reliably collecting relevant government, regulatory, policy, media and expert sources across Japan and, over time, Northeast Asia.
2. **Translation and summarisation** — turning original-language material into concise English summaries.
3. **Classification** — tagging each item by country, institution, issue, sector, urgency, relevance and source type.
4. **Archive** — storing every item and brief in a searchable, reusable knowledge base.
5. **Source discipline** — ensuring claims can be traced back to original or trusted source material.
6. **Analytical format** — consistently answering: what happened, why it matters, what changed, and what to watch next.
7. **Feedback loop** — learning which items are useful, which are noise, and which deserve deeper treatment.
8. **Output flexibility** — using the same system to generate daily briefs, weekly summaries, issue trackers, source packs, risk notes, client-style memos or future paid products.

## Not Yet a Fixed Product

The key idea is that Pacific Vector is not yet a fixed product. It is an intelligence production system. The daily brief is simply the first visible output.

Over time, repeated use of the system should reveal the best commercial opportunity. That might be a professional newsletter, a policy-monitoring service, a regulatory change tracker, a deal-risk memo product, a source-backed briefing engine, or a proprietary dataset.

## The Discipline

The discipline is to keep building the machinery while making each daily brief genuinely useful. Every feature should improve either the quality of the brief, the structure of the archive, or the ability to generate future intelligence products.

## In Short

Pacific Vector is an intelligence workshop. The daily brief is the first output. The system behind it is the asset.
